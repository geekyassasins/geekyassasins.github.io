Tell us about the company in which you have been placed.

I got placed in Tata Steel. It is a pre-placement offer which I received after an internship there through the campus selection that we had. It's quite an amicable company and one can say that it is some kind of a pseudo government job. They have a very amiable working culture where everyone is at par when it comes to work, be it an intern or a chief. For instance, if you have an advisement concerning a technical issue, they will definitely hearken.


Tell us about the selection procedure at that company?

I was first selected as an intern. The duration of the internship was two months. The process here for selection of an intern consisted of three rounds, the first one was a written round, then the group discussion stage and then finally the technical interview. The written test inhered aptitude, technical and logical reasoning section, usually technical section is given more weightage. I scored 100 percentile in the technical section. The questions in technical section were quite random, they ranged from Machines, EEGC, Instrumentation and Power systems.


How important is CGPA a factor in placement?

CGPA apprise how good you are as a student, a paradigm of how well you used to perform on paper, so having a good CGPA reflects that you were quite disciplined about your studies and you gave importance to them. On the contrary if you have a mediocre GPA it means that you have given your time to do something else and if that something else is related to some skillset helpful in placements then you may have an advantage or else you certainly are at loss. To ensure cutoff’s of most companies, CGPA 7.5 and above can be considered good.

Do club activities in general impact placement?

One thing which is earnest during interview is the demeanor that you have, as well as your disposition towards the interview. These things don’t come to you impromptu, and so club activities do help you to become versed with these things.


What does it take for an EEE undergrad to be placed in software/coding based company?

Our curriculum allows us to explore many options, as we had C and data structures courses common for all in first year. So anybody who realizes that he/she has his/her interests in coding and software based sphere by third semester, has to start learning OS, DBMS and other relevant courses of their interest on his/her own. ACM contests, NPTEL, coursera videos & other online sources are best utilized by such students. We have DC to help in this regard.


What topics are generally given for Group Discussions in interviews?

It depends on company to company but usually group discussion is done on socio- political issues or if it is technical company they might give you a technical case study. For a few companies,being decisive in arguments is a needed skill. They would give a squabbling topic and ask a group to assert among themselves and come to a unanimous decision.


Say one has published a technical paper. Does it have tangible benefits when it comes to placements?

It depends on the company you applied for and job profile they offer. Showing papers in resume for irrelevant companies/profiles will only backfire. Most of the companies that offer profiles like R&D or that demand quality skillset & good experience, boast about their intellectual property. A relevant research paper / patent for such will give you an edge and boost chances of acceptance.
