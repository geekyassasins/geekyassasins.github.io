Enigma

1.Description:In 1805,Samuel Morse invented what was going to be the most groundbreaking communication system ever,the Morse code and telegraph.The electric telegraph transformed how wars were fought and won and how journalists and newspapers conducted business.Rather than taking weeks to be delivered by horse and carriage mail carts,pieces of news could be exchanged between telegraph stations almost instantly.This code uses small and long electric pulses to generate "dots" and "dashes" which are used in different combinations to represent alphabets.This event will you get into the shoes of Samuel Morse and Sherlock Holmes at the same time as you design a circuit which can generate Morse code and then decode the code and try to guess what your partner has got out of the message box.
2.RulesFirst the team of two contestants have to make a circuit consisting of 2 led's and two pushbuttons(or they can tap the wires) One LED will be to show the dots and dashes and the other will be lighted up to show the end of the word

The RAF recruits from all STEM disciplines and is the UK’s aerial, peacekeeping and fighting force. Find out more »

January Prize - 4 winners

January’s prize consists of a visit to RAF Coningsby to meet senior engineering officers and sit in the cockpit of a Typhoon fighter plane, ‘the world's most advanced fighter aircraft’.

On the day

You’ll be picked up on the RAF bus from Grantham Train Station and taken to Cranwell, where you’ll meet the RAF Engineer recruiter. Then, you’ll travel to RAF Coningsby, and meet its Chief Engineering Officer.

After lunch in the Officers' Mess, an Aerosystems Engineering Officer from 29 Squadron will introduce you to the Typhoon aircraft, and you’ll even get to sit in its cockpit! You’ll then meet a Communications Electronics Engineer Officer from the Ground Support Systems Flight.

Afterwards, you’ll meet a Communications Electronics Engineer Officer from 90 Signals Unit. They’ll talk about how they provide the deployed communications and data networks that keep everything functioning. You’ll then return to Grantham Station for 4pm.

More Details

  • Your travel expenses will be paid for.
  • If you have far to travel, then the option to stay at Cranwell the night before or after the visit is available.
  • The actual date of the event will be by mutual agreement between The RAF and the prize winners.
  • Gradcracker will provide the prize winners with more details after the draw has been made.

March Prize
Terms and conditions
Lendlease recruits from all STEM disciplines and is a leading international property and infrastructure group. Find out more »

March Prize - 2 Winners

This month’s prize, for two lucky winners (plus a friend each), is to enjoy a tailored tour of Lendlease’s International Quarter London (IQL) project, a boat trip through the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, and a slide down the ArcelorMittal Orbit!

On the day

Your experience will start at 11:30am, when you’ll meet senior members and graduates of the IQL project at their office and enjoy lunch, provided by Lendlease, together. This will be followed by a tour of the project, which can be tailored to your interests and studies.

Then, at 3pm, you’ll enjoy a boat tour of the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park (subject to weather), exploring the site that became so iconic during the summer of 2012. At 4:30pm, you’ll be offered the chance to experience the ArcelorMittal Orbit - the UK’s tallest sculpture, and the world’s longest tunnel slide.

More Details

  • Lendlease will pay train and London Underground expenses to and from the location.
  • The actual date of the event will be by mutual agreement between Lendlease and the prize winner.
  • Gradcracker will provide the prize winners with more details after the draw has been made.

3M recruits from all STEM disciplines and 1.7 billion people use their products every day. Find out more »

November 2017 Prize

3M is a technology company that prides itself on innovation. This innovation enables them to create new technologies to help make the world a safer and healthier place.

On the day

Your day will start at the train station, where you’ll hop onto the free 3M Shuttle Bus to their site for 10.30am. Once you arrive, you’ll be given a general introduction to 3M, followed by a tour around the Customer Innovation Centre.

You’ll then get the chance to have lunch with R&D experts, with the opportunity to ask questions about their role, what it’s like to work for 3M, and any tips they might have for applying. Afterwards, you’ll be able to visit the laboratories to watch the team and their products in action.

At 3.30pm, you’ll get the Shuttle Bus back to the train station.

Leonardo recruits from all STEM disciplines and operates from 6 locations across the UK. Find out more »

October 2017 Prize Details

Leonardo’s Helicopter Division manages the design, development, test and production of the most complete range of rotary aircraft available for commercial, public utility, security and defence use.

On the day

You’ll be picked up at the train station in Yeovil and whisked off to Leonardo’s base. Then, at 11am, you’ll meet the recruitment team and current graduates. You’ll be able to ask them questions about the company, find out what it’s like to be a graduate at Leonardo, and learn some useful hints and tips for applying.

After lunch, supplied by Leonardo, you’ll be offered a tour of the build line followed by an opportunity to sample a flight on one of their latest simulators.

The day will end at 3pm, when you’ll get a taxi back to the train station.
