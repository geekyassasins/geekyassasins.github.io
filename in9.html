4. Tell us about your selection process.
After the end of third year I went for summer internship in Silicon Mentor. I had an interview over Skype and then a telephonic interview. They did not ask much technical questions. I guess they just wanted to check how interested I was about image processing. There were around 30 summer interns at Silicon Mentor. We were allotted projects in groups of three or four. At the end, three or four interns were offered a PPO. I am glad that I was amongst the selected ones. In seventh semester, I went for Grey Orange for two important reasons. Firstly, it is a startup. Secondly, it allowed EEE students for the profile of firmware engineer which involved image processing. There were three rounds other than Personal Interview. Firstly it was aptitude round which had questions from electronics and microcontrollers. Next was coding round which had three questions and it lasted for an hour. We could use any language for our solution provided the solution should pass all the test cases. After shortlisting around fourteen people in the penultimate round, a single long question was given. We had to suggest the answer in form of pseudo code using the concepts of microcontrollers and image processing. Six of us were shortlisted after this for Personal Interview. It was an intense interview session over telephone. I thoroughly enjoyed it. However, I could not make it through. Next, I appeared for Aakash Institute mostly because of my love for teaching and my good grasp over concepts of Mathematics at 10+2 level. It was a cake walk. However, this was not what I dreamt of.
I kept applying in startups right from the beginning of seventh semester. I donâ€™t remember a single website which I had not explored while looking up for the kind of job that I wanted. It was in eighth semester when I got a call from Tech Robotics saying they wanted to interview me. And to my surprise, I got a call at around 2.30 a.m. for the interview. They asked me about the research paper which I did in the summer internship previous year, questions about things I did in four years of engineering. It went good and they said that I will be informed later. The very next morning they mailed me a project which has to be implemented in OpenCV and I had to update them with my daily progress. And to add to my woes, I was not that proficient in Open CV till then. It required a marathon effort to complete the task and luckily I pulled it off. I remember slogged hours, worked day in and day out to complete the task.
